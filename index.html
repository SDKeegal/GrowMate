Project Scope

Literature Survey

Agriculture is a cornerstone of Sri Lanka’s economy, contributing over 7% to the Gross Domestic Product (GDP) and serving as the main source of income for rural populations. Despite its importance, traditional farming practices are hindered by inefficient resource use, poor production, and environmental challenges. Conventional soil testing and disease diagnosis methods are costly, time-consuming, and often inaccessible to small-scale farmers, who form the majority in Sri Lanka’s agricultural sector.


Recent advancements in IoT and artificial intelligence have enabled innovative solutions for real-time soil monitoring and crop disease detection. IoT-based sensors can continuously measure soil moisture, pH, temperature, and nutrient levels, providing timely data for decision-making. Deep learning models, such as Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), have demonstrated high accuracy in image-based plant disease identification, enabling early intervention and reducing crop losses. However, existing systems often focus on either soil monitoring or disease detection, rarely integrating both functions, and lack multilingual support and user-friendliness for small-scale farmers.


This research aims to address these gaps by developing an integrated AI-based system for soil monitoring and crop disease detection. The proposed system leverages IoT sensors, advanced machine learning models, and a multilingual decision support system to provide actionable insights for farmers, enhancing agricultural productivity and sustainability in Sri Lanka.

Research Gap

Lack of Integrated Solutions: Most current systems focus on either soil monitoring or disease detection, but not both. There is a critical need for integrated solutions that provide comprehensive insights for farmers.

Limited Accessibility: Existing technologies are not accessible to small-scale farmers due to high costs, complex interfaces, and lack of multilingual support.

Technological Limitations: Many solutions require reliable internet connectivity and power, which are not always available in rural and remote agricultural areas.

Absence of Predictive Analytics: Few systems incorporate predictive modeling or machine learning for proactive crop management and disease prevention.

Power and Sustainability Issues: Current systems lack sustainable power sources, limiting their long-term operation in remote locations.

Inadequate Early Warning Systems: There is a gap in systems that provide timely alerts for soil health issues and disease outbreaks, which are crucial for preventing crop losses.

Research Problem & Solution

The Challenge

Sri Lankan agriculture faces low productivity, resource wastage, and recurrent crop losses due to soil degradation and plant diseases.

Traditional soil testing and disease diagnosis methods are expensive, time-consuming, and inaccessible to most farmers, especially smallholders. The lack of real-time data and early warning systems results in inefficient resource use and significant crop losses, threatening food security and economic stability.

Our Solution: AI-Based Soil & Crop Monitoring System

An intelligent, integrated system for real-time soil monitoring and crop disease detection.

The proposed system combines IoT-based sensors for soil health monitoring with AI-driven image analysis for disease detection. It features a multilingual decision support system that provides actionable recommendations to farmers in their local language. The system is designed to be affordable, user-friendly, and sustainable, supporting continuous operation even in remote areas with limited infrastructure. By leveraging real-time data and predictive analytics, the system enables farmers to optimize resource use, prevent disease outbreaks, and improve crop yields, ultimately contributing to sustainable and resilient agriculture in Sri Lanka.

Methodology

The AI-based soil monitoring and crop disease detection system is systematically developed to address the complex problems faced by Sri Lankan agriculture. The system architecture is divided into three critical parts: soil monitoring, disease detection, and decision support.

Key Features

Soil Monitoring

IoT sensors measure soil moisture, pH, temperature, and nutrient content in real time.

Disease Detection

High-resolution cameras capture plant images for CNN-based disease identification.

Sustainable Power

Solar panels and efficient battery management ensure continuous operation in remote areas.

Multilingual DSS

Decision support system provides recommendations in multiple local languages.

Automated Irrigation

Automated irrigation based on real-time soil and weather data optimizes water use.

Early Warning

Timely alerts for soil health issues and disease outbreaks prevent crop losses.

Data Flow Process

  1. IoT sensors and cameras collect soil and plant health data in the field.
  2. Data is transmitted to the central system using low-power, long-range communication.
  3. Machine learning models analyze the data for soil health and disease detection.
  4. Decision support system generates actionable recommendations for farmers.
  5. Recommendations are delivered via a multilingual web or mobile interface.
  6. Automated irrigation and other interventions are triggered as needed.

Technologies Used

The system integrates advanced technologies to ensure efficient soil monitoring and crop disease detection:

Python

Used for data processing, analysis, and implementing machine learning models.

TensorFlow

Deep learning framework for building and training neural network models.

MobileNetV2

Efficient convolutional neural network for image-based disease detection.

Random Forest Classifier

Machine learning algorithm for predictive analytics and decision-making.

Llama 3

State-of-the-art language model for intelligent analysis and recommendations.

Firebase

Cloud platform for real-time data storage, authentication, and backend services.

Flutter

Open-source UI toolkit for building beautiful, natively compiled mobile applications from a single codebase.
